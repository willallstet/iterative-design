Andi Redesign

For this project, our group chose Andi, a startup revitalizing search using generative AI & language models. Like talking with a smart friend, Andi generates simple answers to questions, explaining and summarizing information combined from the best sources. While Andi operates across interfaces, we are designed specifically for their mobile application. Andi is specifically for users who are no longer interested in being the product - specifically people frustrated by endless ads, data tracking, and convoluted search results. This encapsulates a large segment of potential customers, but Andi is likely most relevant to users who are digitally native, frequently use search engines like Google or Yahoo (and are familiar with their layout), and are interested in keeping their online usage private. Andi would change the game - no longer needing to deal with endless adverts polluting a user's digital experience, more direct & accurate answers based on searches (helpful for researchers/frequent searchers), and also a friendlier UI that encourages exploration.

Part 1: Sketching & Wireframing

We conceptualized what we thought Andi could look like in the following sketches:

  1. A logo/persona that talks directly to the user and provides comprehensive, summarized results.
  2. A more typical search engine interface (a text box and search button), with more card/tabulated search results page, each with a brief amount of text and image.
  3. Another traditional search engine interface with a boxed key information results page, starting with an overview.
  4. Search interface led by logo/persona, with mission statement on homepage, and more crowded search results page with top sources text, images, and news.
